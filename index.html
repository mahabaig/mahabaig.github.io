ABOUT ME

WHO I AM
As a kid, I ran around the playground pretending to be a superhero — imagining I was saving people from danger. Today, my battlefield is digital. Cybersecurity gives me that same sense of purpose, only now the threats are real and the mission is to protect people and data from them.

WHAT I DO
Instead of a cape, I use analytics, data, and defense tools to keep individuals and organizations safe from cyberattacks. I focus on threat detection, cloud security, and incident response, driven by curiosity, precision, and integrity — the same mix that turns protection into prevention.

WHAT I’VE DONE
I earned my B.S. in Cybersecurity Management and Analytics from Virginia Tech, graduating Cum Laude, and hold a CompTIA Security+ certification. One of my main projects was building an Azure SOC Lab, where I analyzed over 30,000 Sentinel events, identified brute-force attempts, and visualized attacker activity through geo-dashboards. I’ve also worked with Hydra, Wireshark, and Microsoft Sentinel to test, document, and strengthen system resilience. I’m currently expanding my foundation through Johns Hopkins University’s Applied Generative AI program, using Python to explore AI’s role in automating threat detection and data-driven defense.

WHERE I’M HEADED
I’m focused on growing in cloud security, automation, and AI-powered detection — combining human insight with intelligent technology to build smarter, adaptive defenses. In many ways, I’m still that kid chasing the instinct to protect others — only now I get to do it in the world of cybersecurity.

PROJECTS

Azure SOC Lab

Built a Microsoft Azure Sentinel SOC environment, analyzed 30,000+ events, and visualized global attacks in real time.

Access Control & Security Auditing Lab

Simulated password auditing and post-exploitation with Hydra, FTP login validation, and file retrieval to analyze weak credentials and mitigation strategies.

Network Traffic Analysis & Intrusion Detection Lab

Captured and analyzed live network packets using TCPDump and Wireshark to identify ARP scans and RDP anomalies within the Virginia Tech Cyber Range.
