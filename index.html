About Me

WHO I AM
As a kid, I ran around the playground pretending to be a superhero — imagining I was saving people from danger. Today, my battlefield is digital. Cybersecurity gives me that same sense of purpose, only now the threats are real and the mission is to protect people and data from them.

WHAT I DO
Instead of a cape, I use analytics, data, and defense tools to keep individuals and organizations safe from cyberattacks. I focus on threat detection, cloud security, and incident response, driven by curiosity, precision, and integrity — the same mix that turns protection into prevention.

WHAT I’VE DONE
I earned my B.S. in Cybersecurity Management and Analytics from Virginia Tech, graduating Cum Laude, and hold a CompTIA Security+ certification. One of my main projects was building an Azure SOC Lab, where I analyzed over 30,000 Sentinel events, identified brute-force attempts, and visualized attacker activity through geo-dashboards. I’ve also worked with Hydra, Wireshark, and Microsoft Sentinel to test, document, and strengthen system resilience. Currently, I’m expanding my technical foundation through Johns Hopkins University’s Applied Generative AI program, using Python to explore how artificial intelligence can enhance security automation, detection, and decision-making.

WHERE I’M HEADED
I’m focused on growing in cloud security, automation, and AI-powered detection — combining human insight with intelligent technology to build smarter, adaptive defenses. In many ways, I’m still that kid chasing the instinct to protect others — only now I get to do it in the world of cybersecurity.

This project demonstrates hands-on experience in building a Microsoft Azure SOC environment, analyzing security events, and visualizing global attack data.
